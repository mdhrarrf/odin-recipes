Fried Chicken

Description

Fried chicken is a crispy, golden-brown dish made by marinating chicken in buttermilk, coating it in seasoned flour, and deep-frying it until juicy and tender inside.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, marinate the chicken pieces in buttermilk for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine the flour, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep skillet or fryer to 350°F (175°C).
  4. Remove the chicken from the buttermilk, allowing excess to drip off, and dredge each piece in the seasoned flour mixture, pressing to adhere.
  5. Carefully place the coated chicken pieces in the hot oil and fry until golden brown and cooked through, about 12-15 minutes, depending on the size of the pieces.
  6. Remove the fried chicken from the oil and place on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
