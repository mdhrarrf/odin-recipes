Seblak

Description

Seblak is a popular Indonesian street food known for its bold, spicy, and savory flavor. It is made from softened crackers cooked in a rich broth flavored with garlic, chilies, and aromatic spices. The dish is often combined with eggs, vegetables, and various toppings such as chicken, meatballs, or sausages, creating a comforting and fiery meal loved by spice enthusiasts.

Ingredients

  • 100 grams of kerupuk (Indonesian crackers)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 red chilies, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 1 cup of chicken broth
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1/2 cup of sliced vegetables (e.g., carrots, cabbage)
  • Optional toppings: cooked chicken, meatballs, sausages
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Soften the kerupuk by soaking them in warm water for about 10 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  2. In a pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced chilies, sautéing until fragrant.
  3. Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Add the softened kerupuk and cook for a few minutes until they absorb the flavors of the broth.
  4. Push the kerupuk to one side of the pan and pour the beaten egg into the empty space. Scramble the egg until cooked, then mix it with the kerupuk.
  5. Add the sliced vegetables and optional toppings, cooking until the vegetables are tender. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
